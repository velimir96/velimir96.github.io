I am a second year PhD student in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering at UCSD where I am advised by Prof. Miroslav Krstic. I am working on model-free optimization and safe stabilization for dynamical systems with applications ranging from steering mobile robots in GPS-denied environments to seeking of Nash equilibria. More recently, I am developing novel control degins for parking (stabilization) of nonholonomic vehicles, that are simultaneously safe, optimal, and adaptive , as well as park the vehicles in used-defined finite time.


I have obtained a M.Sc. degree in Electrical Engineering from the Technical University of Munich (TUM) where I focused on Automation and Robotics. I have also spent a year as a research assistant at the Chair of Information-oriented Control at TUM. Before that, I did my undergrad in Electrical Engineering at FAU where my bachelor thesis on algorithms for fault diagnosis in dynamical systems was advised by Prof. Joachim Deutscher.
